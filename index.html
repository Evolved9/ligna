LIGNA Furniture Family Concept

Where two opposing materials — wood and steel — find a shared design language within a single form, uniting structure and nature in modular harmony.

Collection

Form & Material,
Reconciled. Steel cage meets living wood.

Ligna is a modular furniture family built on a single paradox: two entirely opposite materials — wood and metal — brought together not in contrast, but in conversation. The welded steel cage provides structure and transparency; the wood panel provides warmth and mass.

Each piece in the collection — the coffee table, the side table, the occasional stool — shares the same constructive logic while offering a distinct spatial character. Together they form a coherent domestic system.

Design Process

Six ideas.
One resolution.

The design process began with a structural question: how can a welded wire cage and a curved wood panel coexist not as separate elements, but as a single tectonic unit? Six form studies explored different approaches to this joint — from flush integration to deliberate gap and contrast.

01
Form Studies

Six panel curvature variations explored how wood bends and terminates — from tight radius arches to gradual sweeps — to determine the final structural posture.

02
Cage Construction

Two wire grid patterns — diagonal lattice and vertical bar — were tested for visual weight, shadow quality, and structural logic before finalizing the welded cage geometry.

03
Surface Language

Four surface studies defined the panel's material character: textured wood, open cage, wood-over-cage layering, and a textured repeat — establishing the visual rhythm of the collection.

A form supported by a strong grid structure, completed by natural textures.

Ligna is a modular furniture collection that adapts to the changing needs of its users — combining function and aesthetics through an honest dialogue between industrial steel and organic wood. Each configuration recombines the same vocabulary; no piece reads as an afterthought.
